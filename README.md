# Student-Financial-Risk-Analysis
🔎 **Project Overview**

This project analyzes the financial sustainability of international students across major Canadian cities.

The objective is to determine whether students can cover living expenses within the 20-hour/week visa work limit.

Using SQL, Python, and Power BI, this project identifies cities where students are at high financial risk due to insufficient income relative to cost of living.

**🎯 Business Problem**

International students in Canada are typically limited to working 20 hours per week during academic sessions.

However, rising rent and living costs may require significantly more working hours to break even.

This project answers:

How many hours per week are required to cover living expenses?

Which cities exceed the 20-hour visa limit?

How many cities fall into high financial risk?

Which cities have the highest financial deficit?

**🛠 Tools & Technologies**

SQL (MySQL) – Data modeling and querying

Python (Pandas) – Data cleaning and transformation

Power BI – Data visualization and dashboard creation

Numbeo dataset – Cost of living data

**📂 Data Model**

The dataset includes:

City

Total Living Cost

Minimum Wage

Monthly Income

Surplus / Deficit

Hours Needed Per Week

Risk Level (High / Medium / Low)

**📈 Key Metrics**

📌 Average Hours Required Per Week: 34.8 hours

📌 Average Monthly Living Cost: $2.2K

📌 High-Risk Cities: 3

📌 Students require ~14.8 extra hours beyond visa limit

**🚨 Key Insights**

Students need on average 34.8 hours/week, exceeding the 20-hour visa limit.

3 cities fall into the high financial risk category.

Vancouver and Toronto require the highest working hours.

Quebec City shows the lowest required working hours.

Majority of cities fall under medium financial risk.
